1890

Birth

Agatha Mary Clarissa Miller, born on 15 September 1890 in Torquay, Devon, to wealthy parents Frederick Alvah Miller and Clarissa Margaret "Clara" Miller, n√©e Boehmer, was the youngest of three children.

1901

Childhood

As an adolescent, she enjoyed works by Anthony Hope, Walter Scott, Charles Dickens, and Alexandre Dumas.In April 1901, aged 10, she wrote her first poem, "The Cow Slip".

1908

Prelude

At 18, recovering from illness, Christie wrote her debut story, "The House of Beauty," exploring "madness and dreams," topics of fascination. Concurrently, she initiated her initial novel, "Snow Upon the Desert."

1912

Love

In October 1912, she was introduced to Archibald "Archie" Christie at a dance,the couple quickly fell in love. Three months after their first meeting, Archie proposed marriage, and Agatha accepted.

1926

Vanishing

In August,Archie requested a divorce and Christie vanished from their Sunningdale home.On December,she was found at the Swan Hydropathic Hotel in Harrogate,diagnosed with an "unquestionable genuine loss of memory."

1934

Epitome

In 1934, Christie used her international train travels for Murder on the Orient Express. The Pera Palace Hotel in Istanbul, the railway's eastern terminus, claims the book's authorship, preserving Christie's room as a memorials.

1976

Death

Christie peacefully passed away at 85 on January 12 at Winterbrook House. The St. Martin's and the Savoy, staging The Mousetrap and a revival of Murder at the Vicarage, dimmed outside lights in her honor.
