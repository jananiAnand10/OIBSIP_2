Agatha Christie Exhibition

Royal Mail Commemoration

Royal Mail issued six first-class postage stamps in 2016 to mark the centenary of Christie's first detective story

International Recognition

In 2020, the Royal Mint issued a £2 coin commemorating Christie, marking the centenary of her first novel, The Mysterious Affair at Styles.

Bronze Statue in Wallingford

In 2023, a life-size bronze statue of Agatha Christie, seated on a park bench holding a book, was unveiled in Wallingford.

Film and Television Adaptations

Christie's works have been adapted for cinema and TV since the 1928 film "The Passing of Mr. Quin."

Television Series

"Agatha Christie's Poirot" (1989–2013) featured David Suchet and ran for 70 episodes, winning several BAFTA awards.

Other Adaptations

Christie's books have been adapted for BBC Radio, video games, and graphic novels
